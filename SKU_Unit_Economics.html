Key Observations — M-o-M Unit Economics
BB Roll On 25ml — Consistent improvement, portfolio anchor
Ops% fell from 22.5% (March) → 17.3% (Jun 1-14). Inward dropped $0.68→$0.48; outward $4.08→$3.65. COGS only $0.75 (3%). At $24.65 ASP, GM = 79.7%. Scale this SKU.
Alpha 60s — Recovering from 34.4% in March
Ops% down 7.6 ppt over 4 months (34.4% → 26.8%). Inward improving ($2.80 → $2.52). Outward still high at $4.37/u. FBT push should get to 23–24% target.
BB Roll On 50ml — Best unit economics in portfolio
Ops% 12.6%, GM 83.2%. ASP $37.04. Very low ops cost per unit. Declining volume (3,734 → 988 units in 14 days) is the only risk. Multi-pack + FBT = volume recovery lever.
MagAshwa — Improved from 52.6% in March but still critical at 36.7%
Was catastrophically high in March (52.6%!). Came down via inward reduction ($6.94 → $5.12). But still 10 ppt above Alpha 60s. Root cause: volumetric weight penalty on carton. Fix now = another ~10 ppt drop.
Seamoss 60s — Volatile, at-risk SKU
Ops% oscillates: 38.3% March → 28.2% May (great!) → 36.6% Jun (back up). ASP only $19.36. This SKU has no pricing headroom to absorb ops cost swings. Consider price increase or exit.
BB HGS Roll On 25ml — Carrier minimum trap
Volume fell 849 → 92 units in 14 days. Outward/unit went $2.09 → $4.71 because carrier minimum charge kicks in below ~300 units/fortnight. If volume stays below 200/fortnight: consider discontinuation or merge onto BB Roll On 25ml carrier lane.
MagAshwa — Ops% Lever Analysis
Current state: 36.7% ops% — worst in portfolio by 10+ ppt. At 5,520 units in 14 days (~11,040/month), this is costing an extra ~$114K/month in ops vs if it performed like Alpha 60s (26.8%). The inward alone ($5.12/unit) is the single biggest lever — 2× higher than Alpha 60s ($2.52).

Multi-Unit Strategy — Why Bundles Improve Ops%
Multi-unit packs (Pack of 2) improve economics by: (1) amortising outward logistics over more units, (2) raising ASP which dilutes fixed inward cost %. Key metric: ops% should drop significantly on Pack of 2 vs single.
How multi-unit packs work in ops:
Single unit: 1 unit shipped → 1× outward fulfillment fee (e.g. $4.37 for Alpha 60s).
Pack of 2: 2 units shipped together → 1× fulfillment fee split across 2 units = $2.19 effective outward per unit.
Net result: outward% of revenue drops ~50% for the bundled units, often improving ops% by 5–8 ppt.
